Accidental Inventions

There are some inventions that we use on a daily basis. Also these inventions have been very useful to us in many ways. Ranging from satisying your tastebuds to saving lives. In this webpage we will see the top 5 popular accidental inventions. This webpage is for the Contest: Inventions - HTML Submissions.

Here goes a list of accidental inventions. There are many more accidental inventions but these are some of popular ones. I hope you find them interesting! You can click on the icons for more information.

  1. Sticky notes

    2. sticky_note_2

    Sticky notes are something that we use a lot. A 3M scientist Dr. Spencer Silver was doing research on strong adhesives when he came across quite the reverse: one that stuck lightly to surfaces but didn't bond tightly to them. Years later another 3M scientist, Art Fry, came to him to create a bookmark that could stick to paper without damaging it. Eventually, that bookmark became the post-it note.

  2. Chocolate chip cookies

    3. cookie

    Ruth Wakefield and her husband owned and operated the Toll House Inn in Massachusetts where Ruth cooked for the guests. According to legend, one day in 1937 while making cookie dough, she realized she was out of melting baker’s chocolate and instead used a chocolate bar that she chopped into bits, hoping it would melt as well. It didn’t, and thus was born America's favorite cookie.

  3. Penicillin

    4. mixture_med

    Penicillin- the most famous lifesaving discovery. Discovered in 1928, Penicillin was one of the world's first antibiotics. Alexander Fleming, a Scottish scientist, found a mold in one of the petri dishes containing the staphylococci bacteria after leaving it for a few days in the open. The mold had formed a sterile shield around itself killing the bacteria in the dish. He decided to study further on these bacteria and hence invented penicillin.

  4. The X-ray Machine

    5. rib_cage

    On November 8, 1895, physicist Wilhelm Conrad Rontgen was in his laboratory in Wurzburg, Germany, experimenting on a vacuum tube covered in cardboard when he noticed a mysterious glow emanating from a chemically coated screen nearby. Confused and intrigued, he named the new rays causing this glow X-rays due to their unknown origin—and after playing around some more with the new rays, he discovered that putting his hand in front of the glow allowed him to see past his skin to his bones, thus leading to the world's first X-ray.

  5. Ice cream cone

    6. icecream

    In 1904 at St. Louis World's Fair, an ice cream vendor ran out of dishes to serve his ice cream in, the vendor next to him named Ernest A. Hamwi, came up with the idea to shape his crêpes into cones as vessels for the frozen treat. Thus was born the ice cream cone. Do you like ice creams in cones or cups?
